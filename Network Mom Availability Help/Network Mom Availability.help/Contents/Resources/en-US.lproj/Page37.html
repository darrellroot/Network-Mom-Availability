Daily and Weekly Reports

Daily reports are emailed automatically once per day. One email per map per report recipient.

Weekly reports are emailed each Monday, immediately after emailing the daily reports. In addition to the report, this includes a text export of the devices monitored.

Monthly reports are emailed at the beginning of each month.

It is also possible to use the  Reports -> Daily/Weekly/Monthly Map Availability Report  menu item to generate a current report to the screen. This report can be copy/pasted into an email, but does not include the availability graph (known issue).

The daily report includes the following sections:

  • Header: This shows the map name, and start and end of the time period for the report.

  • Availability: This includes an “overall availability” score for the map for the entire period, followed by a chart of the availability per hour or per day (depending on whether the report is daily, weekly, or monthly).

  • Bottom-5 availability: This shows the 5 monitors with the worst availability.   Fix these and your availability will improve!

  • Top-5 availability: This shows the 5 monitors with the best availability. If your best availabilities are significantly below 100%, it is possible you have a global problem or a problem near your monitoring station.

  • Monitors which have never responded: These “BLUE STATUS” devices are not included in your availability calculations. It is possible these monitors are misconfigured (wrong IP address?), or they may not respond to pings at all. These are worth cleaning up.

  • 5 monitors with largest recent average latency increase: If you are monitoring a WAN, you want to keep an eye on latency increases. Your ISP may have rerouted your circuits to a suboptimal path ( worth fixing ). Normal fluctuations can cause wide discrepancies in this arithmetic average of the latency. LAN devices with small (absolute) latency may appear to suffer large percentage increases which have no operational impact (1msec to 2msec,  not worth fixing ). The latency report is useful but needs human judgement.

  • Availability graph: This graph repeats the data in the availability chart (#2) visually. It is only included in email reports, not menu-triggered reports (known issue). The bottom of the availability scale is determined by the lowest availability data point. It could be 0% (zero nines), 90% (one nine), 99% (two nines), 99.9% (three nines) or 99.99% (four nines).
